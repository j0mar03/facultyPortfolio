# **SUMMARY OF FINDINGS, CONCLUSIONS, AND RECOMMENDATIONS**

This chapter presents the summary of findings, conclusions, and recommendations based on the development and post-implementation evaluation of the Faculty Portfolio Management System (FPMS).

## Summary of Findings

The study aimed to develop and evaluate a role-based web-based FPMS for higher education compliance workflows. Using operational records and survey responses (`N=35`), the following findings were obtained:

1. **Post-implementation workflow profile was highly acceptable in user perception, with partial objective log evidence.**
   - Workflow Improvement (WI) mean was `4.53` (SD=`0.21`), and Review Turnaround Support (RT) mean was `4.53` (SD=`0.17`), both interpreted as Highly Acceptable.
   - Mean processing duration from available logs was `14,582.01` minutes for submitted/decided records (`n=29`).
   - Objective review-turnaround was measurable at `23,712.91` minutes mean (`n=29`), indicating a delay-sensitive stage requiring workload and process follow-up.

2. **Post-implementation compliance and governance profile was highly acceptable, with measurable completeness outcomes.**
   - Completeness Support (CS) mean was `4.75` (SD=`0.19`) and Governance/Traceability (GT) mean was `4.30` (SD=`0.17`), both Highly Acceptable.
   - Operational records showed `17.90%` complete submissions (`29/162`) and `82.10%` incomplete submissions (`133/162`).
   - Role-based governance controls were implemented, while action-level traceability remained partial due to unavailable `audit_logs` inserts in the selected backup.

3. **Usability and user acceptance were positive, with usability at a marginal acceptable level.**
   - SUS overall score was `62.00` (SD=`2.25`), interpreted as Marginal/Acceptable.
   - TAM constructs were positive: PU=`4.17` (SD=`0.30`), PEOU=`4.17` (SD=`0.17`), BI=`4.26` (SD=`0.21`), with overall TAM mean around `4.19`.

4. **Reliability results were mixed and indicate instrument refinement needs.**
   - Overall TAM internal consistency was acceptable (`alpha=0.781`), and PU reliability was strong (`alpha=0.896`).
   - Several construct-level alphas (WI, CS, RT, GT, PEOU, BI, and SUS raw alpha) were low or negative, indicating that those subscale findings are exploratory/descriptive in this cycle rather than strong psychometric confirmation.
   - Item-level response clustering and limited variance in selected indicators likely contributed to depressed alpha values.

## Conclusions

Based on the findings, the study concludes that:

1. **The developed FPMS is a practical and deployable governance-oriented platform for faculty portfolio workflows in higher education.**
   The system operationalizes role-based workflow control, required-document support, and compliance-oriented record structuring.

2. **FPMS demonstrates strong perceived effectiveness for workflow and compliance/governance support.**
   High WI, RT, CS, and GT means indicate that users experienced improved organization, status visibility, and governance support after implementation.

3. **FPMS achieved acceptable user acceptance with usable but improvable interface quality.**
   TAM results indicate positive adoption intention and perceived value, while SUS suggests that usability improvement remains necessary for stronger user experience.

4. **Current operational evidence is sufficient for initial implementation claims but not for full review-cycle and audit-event claims.**
   Review records were measurable in the updated extraction, but action-level audit-event evidence remained unavailable (`audit_logs` not populated), limiting full governance-traceability claims.

5. **The current instrument and evaluation design should be strengthened in subsequent cycles.**
   Mixed reliability results indicate the need to refine item wording/structure, expand sample variability, and improve measurement robustness for future validation; therefore, construct-level claims with weak alpha should be interpreted cautiously.

## Recommendations

Based on the results and conclusions, the following are recommended:

1. **Institutional implementation and policy use**
   Continue operational deployment of FPMS as the centralized platform for faculty portfolio compliance workflows.

2. **Workflow and governance data completeness**
   Enforce complete reviewer action logging and audit-log activation so that future evaluations can compute objective review-turnaround and action-level traceability metrics.

3. **Usability enhancement**
   Improve interface clarity, in-system guidance, and task flow to raise SUS performance beyond marginal-acceptable levels.

4. **Instrument refinement and psychometric quality**
   Revise low-performing WI/CS/RT/GT/PEOU/BI items, conduct item analysis (item-total correlation and alpha-if-item-deleted), check reverse-coded handling, and revalidate reliability with a larger and more balanced respondent sample.

5. **Expanded evaluation scope**
   Replicate the study across additional departments/campuses and multiple terms to strengthen external validity and longitudinal evidence.

6. **Reporting and submission readiness**
   Maintain strict evidence alignment between Abstract, Results, Discussion, and Conclusion, and ensure final manuscript compliance with BTEMS formatting and similarity threshold requirements.

## Suggested Future Research

1. Develop and validate predictive analytics modules for early detection of at-risk incomplete submissions.
2. Evaluate the impact of automated reminders and escalation workflows on completeness and turnaround indicators.
3. Compare cross-department implementation outcomes to identify contextual adoption factors.
4. Conduct longitudinal studies linking FPMS usage patterns to compliance performance trends over academic terms.
